She's Dating the Gangster

by Bianca Bernardino

In She’s Dating the Gangster by Bianca Bernardino, the protagonist is Athena Dizon. She is a simple and kind student who gets caught in a fake relationship with Kenji Delos Reyes, a rebellious boy known for his temper. Her patience and sincerity balance Kenji’s rough attitude. As the story progresses, Athena shows strength and maturity through her experiences with love and pain. Athena experiences both emotional and relational conflicts. At first, she agrees to pretend to be Kenji’s girlfriend to make his ex jealous, but she slowly develops real feelings for him. She struggles with confusion, guilt, and heartbreak when their relationship becomes serious. Her biggest conflict is between following her emotions and protecting herself from more pain. The climax happens when Athena and Kenji realize they truly love each other but cannot be together because of Kenji’s illness and personal struggles. This moment defines the emotional weight of the story. It turns their playful start into a serious and painful reality, showing how love can change people. The setting strengthens the story’s emotions. The school represents youth, fun, and first love, while the hospital represents loss, maturity, and acceptance. The change in setting mirrors the shift from a lighthearted romance to a deeper, more emotional relationship. The theme centers on love, sacrifice, and personal growth. The story shows that real love is not always happy but teaches responsibility and strength. It reminds you that maturity often comes from pain. The characters, plot, and setting develop together. Athena and Kenji grow through love and hardship. The plot moves from playful to serious, reflecting emotional change. The settings support this growth, showing how love evolves with time and experience. Together, they form a complete story about how people learn and change through love.
